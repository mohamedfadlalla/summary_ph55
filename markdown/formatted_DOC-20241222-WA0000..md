# **2. Drug/Class Coverage**

### **Drug/Class: Insulin**
*   **Drug Name:** <span style="color:blue">Insulin</span>
*   **Drug Class:** Pancreatic Hormone (Anabolic)
*   **Mechanism of Action:** <span style="color:purple">Binds to a specific tyrosine kinase-linked receptor (2α and 2β subunits). This triggers autophosphorylation of the β subunits, recruiting Insulin Receptor Substrate (IRS) proteins. Initiates signaling cascades (Ras-MAPK) to regulate glucose transporters (GLUT-4 translocation), metabolic enzymes, and gene expression.</span>
*   **Pharmacokinetics:** Synthesized as preproinsulin → proinsulin → insulin + C-peptide. Half-life is short; stored as crystals consisting of two zinc atoms and six insulin molecules (hexamer).
*   **Pharmacodynamics:** Stimulates glucose uptake in muscle and adipose tissue via GLUT-4; promotes glycogenesis; inhibits glycogenolysis/gluconeogenesis; increases lipogenesis; stimulates protein synthesis.
*   **Clinical Uses:** <span style="color:green">Management of Type 1 DM, Type 2 DM (when oral agents are insufficient), and Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM).</span>
*   **Indications:** Hyperglycemia, Type 1 Diabetes, Type 2 Diabetes, Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA), Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar State.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypoglycemia.
*   **Adverse Effects:** <span style="color:red">Hypoglycemia, weight gain, lipodystrophy at injection site.</span>
*   **Drug Interactions:** <span style="color:orange">Beta-blockers (may mask hypoglycemia symptoms), Corticosteroids (increase blood glucose).</span>
*   **Toxicity:** <span style="color:red">Severe hypoglycemia leading to coma or seizures.</span>
*   **Clinical Manifestations:** <span style="color:red">Sweating, palpitations, tremor, hunger, confusion (hypoglycemia).</span>
*   **Clinical Management:** Administration of glucose (oral or IV) or glucagon.
*   **Monitoring Parameters:** Blood glucose levels (SMBG), HbA1c (every 3-6 months).
*   **Dose(s):** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Route of Administration:** Subcutaneous (common), Intravenous (emergency).
*   **Important Notes:** <span style="color:orange">Insulin is the preferred medication for GDM as it does not cross the placenta.</span> It is cosecreted with C-peptide in equimolar amounts (used to measure endogenous insulin production).

### **Drug/Class: Amylin (Islet Amyloid Polypeptide)**
*   **Drug Name:** <span style="color:blue">Amylin (IAPP)</span>
*   **Drug Class:** Pancreatic Peptide Hormone (B-cell product)
*   **Mechanism of Action:** <span style="color:purple">Cosecreted with insulin (1:100 ratio). Prevents post-prandial rise in blood glucose by slowing gastric emptying, promoting satiety, and inhibiting glucagon secretion.</span>
*   **Pharmacokinetics:** Cosecreted with insulin from B (β) cells.
*   **Pharmacodynamics:** Modulates rate of glucose appearance in the blood.
*   **Clinical Uses:** <span style="color:green">Glycemic regulation and post-prandial glucose control.</span>
*   **Indications:** Type 1 and Type 2 Diabetes (as adjunct therapy).
*   **Contraindications:** Gastroparesis (due to slowed gastric emptying).
*   **Adverse Effects:** <span style="color:red">Nausea, vomiting, hypoglycemia (when used with insulin).</span>
*   **Drug Interactions:** Drugs that affect gastrointestinal motility.
*   **Toxicity:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Manifestations:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Management:** Dose adjustment of concurrent insulin.
*   **Monitoring Parameters:** Post-prandial glucose.
*   **Dose(s):** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Route of Administration:** Subcutaneous.
*   **Important Notes:** Plays a synergistic role with insulin in maintaining euglycemia.

### **Drug/Class: Glucagon**
*   **Drug Name:** <span style="color:blue">Glucagon</span>
*   **Drug Class:** Pancreatic Hormone (Counter-regulatory/Hyperglycemic)
*   **Mechanism of Action:** <span style="color:purple">Mobilizes hepatic glycogen stores (glycogenolysis) to increase blood glucose levels.</span>
*   **Pharmacokinetics:** Produced by Alpha (A) cells in the islets of Langerhans.
*   **Pharmacodynamics:** Acts as a physiological antagonist to insulin.
*   **Clinical Uses:** <span style="color:green">Emergency treatment of severe hypoglycemia.</span>
*   **Indications:** Severe hypoglycemia in diabetic patients.
*   **Contraindications:** Pheochromocytoma, Insulinoma.
*   **Adverse Effects:** <span style="color:red">Nausea, vomiting.</span>
*   **Drug Interactions:** May enhance the effect of oral anticoagulants.
*   **Toxicity:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Manifestations:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Management:** Monitor blood glucose immediately after use.
*   **Monitoring Parameters:** Blood glucose response.
*   **Dose(s):** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Route of Administration:** IM, SC, or IV.
*   **Important Notes:** Glucagon levels are inhibited by Amylin and Somatostatin.

### **Drug/Class: Somatostatin**
*   **Drug Name:** <span style="color:blue">Somatostatin</span>
*   **Drug Class:** Pancreatic Hormone (Inhibitory)
*   **Mechanism of Action:** <span style="color:purple">Universal inhibitor of secretory cells. Inhibits the release of glucagon, insulin, growth hormone (GH), and gastrin.</span>
*   **Pharmacokinetics:** Produced by Delta (D) cells. Short half-life.
*   **Pharmacodynamics:** Paracrine and endocrine inhibitory effects.
*   **Clinical Uses:** <span style="color:green">Treatment of acromegaly, carcinoid tumors, and specific diarrheal states (usually via synthetic analogs).</span>
*   **Indications:** Secretory disorders.
*   **Contraindications:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Adverse Effects:** <span style="color:red">Biliary sludge, gallstones, steatorrhea.</span>
*   **Drug Interactions:** May decrease absorption of many orally administered drugs.
*   **Toxicity:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Manifestations:** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Clinical Management:** Monitor for gallbladder disease.
*   **Monitoring Parameters:** Growth hormone levels, glucose levels.
*   **Dose(s):** **Information not provided in original document.**
*   **Route of Administration:** IV or SC.
*   **Important Notes:** Found in 3-5% of islet mass.

# **3. High-Yield Exam Section**

*   **Common MCQ Traps:**
    *   *Trap:* HbA1c is always the gold standard for diagnosis.
    *   *Fact:* <span style="color:orange">HbA1c may be inaccurate in patients with hemolytic anemia, sickle cell disease, or recent blood transfusions</span> because it depends on red blood cell turnover.
    *   *Trap:* Insulin is the only product of Beta cells.
    *   *Fact:* Beta cells also secrete **Amylin** and **C-peptide**.

*   **Frequently Confused Drugs:**
    *   **Insulin vs. Glucagon:** Insulin lowers blood glucose (anabolic), while Glucagon raises it (catabolic/mobilizing).
    *   **Type 1 vs. Type 2 DM:** Type 1 is <span style="color:red">absolute deficiency</span> (destruction); Type 2 is <span style="color:red">resistance + relative deficiency</span>.

*   **Important Dose-Related Facts:**
    *   **75g oral glucose:** The standard dose used for the Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT).
    *   **2 Zinc atoms / 6 Insulin molecules:** The ratio required to form the crystalline hexamer storage form of insulin.

*   **Important Toxicities:**
    *   **Hypoglycemia:** The most common and dangerous acute toxicity of insulin therapy.
    *   **DKA/HHS:** Acute complications of untreated or poorly managed diabetes.

*   **Key Comparisons:**
    *   **Type 1A DM:** Immune-mediated destruction (most common Type 1).
    *   **Type 1B DM:** Idiopathic destruction.
    *   **MODY:** Genetic defect in insulin *secretion*, not action; usually occurs before age 25.

*   **Rapid-Review Bullets:**
    *   **Diagnostic DM Criteria:** FPG **≥ 7.0 mmol/L**, HbA1c **≥ 6.5%**, or Random Glucose **≥ 11.1 mmol/L** with symptoms.
    *   **C-Peptide:** Best marker for endogenous insulin production because it is not present in pharmaceutical insulin.
    *   **GDM Treatment:** <span style="color:orange">Insulin is the drug of choice because it does not cross the placenta.</span>
